Stocks

Stocks are a type of security that gives stockholders a share of ownership in a company. Stocks also are called “equities.”

1.Why do people buy stocks?

There Various reasons why investors buy stocks. Few of them are:

  1. Capital appreciation or the profits incurred by the investors, when a stock rises in price.
  2. Dividend payments, which are paid when the company distributes some of its earnings to stockholders.
  3. Ability to vote shares and influence the company.

2.Why do companies issue stocks?

Companies issue stock to get money for various things, which may include:

  1. Paying off debt.
  2. Launching new products.
  3. Expanding into new markets or regions.
  4. Enlarging facilities or building new ones.

3.What are the different types of stocks?

There are two main kinds of stocks, common stock and preferred stock.

  1. Common stock entitles owners to vote at shareholder meetings and receive dividends.


  2. Preferred stock is a class of stock that is granted certain rights that differ from common stock. Namely, preferred stock often possesses higher dividend payments, and a higher claim to assets in the event of liquidation.Usually these stockholder have no voting rights.

Common and preferred stocks may fall into one or more of the following categories:

  • Growth stocks is any share in a company that is anticipated to grow at a rate significantly above the average growth for the market. These stocks generally do not pay dividends. A start-up technology company is likely to be a growth stock.


  • Income stocks are stocks that offer regular and steady income, usually in the form of dividends, over a period of time with low exposure to risk. An established utility company is likely to be an income stock.


  • Value stocks refers to shares of a company that appears to trade at a lower price relative to its fundamentals, such as dividends, earnings, or sales, making it appealing to value investors. People buy value stocks in the hope that the market has overreacted and that the stock’s price will rebound.


  • Blue-chip stocks are shares of very large and well-recognised companies with a long history of sound financial performance. These stocks are known to have capabilities to endure tough market conditions and give high returns in good market conditions.

Another way to categorize stocks is by the size of the company, as shown in its market capitalization. There are large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks.

4.What are the benefits and risks of stocks?

Benefits
Make money in two ways: Most investors intend to buy low then sell high. They invest in fast-growing companies that appreciate in value.
Liquidity: The stock market allows you to sell your stock at any time.
Easy to buy: The stock market makes it easy to buy shares of companies. You can purchase them through a broker or a financial planner, or online.

Risks
Time Consuming: If you are buying stocks on your own, you must research each company to determine how profitable you think it will be before you buy its stock.
Emotional roller coaster: Stock prices rise and fall second by second. Individuals tend to buy high out of greed, and sell low out of fear.
Taxes: If you sell your stock for a loss, you may be able to get a tax break. However, if you sell your stock for a profit, you'd be liable to to pay capital gains taxes.

5.Where to buy or sell stocks?

Buying or selling stocks can be done through:

  • Direct stock plans, Some companies allow you to buy or sell their stock directly through them without using a broker. This saves on commissions, but you may have to pay other fees to the plan, including if you transfer shares to a broker to sell them.

  • Dividend reinvestment plans, These plans allow you to buy more shares of a stock you already own by reinvesting dividend payments into the company. You must sign an agreement with the company to have this done.

  • Discount or full-service broker, Brokers buy and sell shares for customers for a fee, known as a commission.

  • Stock funds are another way to buy stocks. These are a type of mutual fund that invests primarily in stocks. Depending on its investment objective and policies, a stock fund may concentrate on a particular type of stock, such as blue chips, large-cap value stocks, or mid-cap growth stocks.

6.Stock Market

The term stock market refers to several exchanges in which shares of publicly held companies are bought and sold. Such financial activities are conducted through formal exchanges and via over-the-counter (OTC) marketplaces that operate under a defined set of regulations. Both “stock market” and “stock exchange” are often used interchangeably.

Stock markets provide a secure and regulated environment where market participants can transact in shares and other eligible financial instruments with confidence, with zero to low operational risk. Operating under the defined rules as stated by the regulator, the stock markets act as primary markets and secondary markets

How to invest in the stock market?

Investing in stocks is simple. Follow these steps to begin your investing journey immediately.

  1. Open a Demat and Trading Account. You need trading and Demat accounts to buy and sell shares. Any SEBI-registered broker will allow you to open trading and Demat accounts.

  2. Determine your investment strategy. You have two choices: purchase stocks on the open market or use stock selection services. People prefer to make investments in the stocks of a professionally built portfolio.

  3. Keep an eye on your portfolio. Spend some time periodically checking on your portfolio. Ensure the stock performance matches your expectations by observing it.
